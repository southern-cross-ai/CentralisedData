House debates

Monday, 24 February 2014

Questions without Notice

Employment

2:08 pm

Bill Shorten (Maribyrnong, Australian Labor Party, Leader of the Opposition) | Hansard source

My question is to the Prime Minister. Since the Abbott government was elected, thousands of jobs have been lost: at Toyota, Holden, Forge in Western Australia , Alcoa in Geelong and Rio Tinto in Gove. Why hasn't the Prime Minister had the courage to visit these workers in their communities? Why is the Prime Minister a wimp when it comes to standing up for Australian jobs?

Christopher Pyne (Sturt, Liberal Party, Minister for Education) | Hansard source

Madam Speaker, I rise on a point of order. The Leader of the Opposition's question is out of order. It contains epithets and argument, words that do not have any place in a question. If he wants to ask a sensible, straightforward question, I am sure it will be entertained by the House. But this is merely argument masquerading as a question, and I would ask you to rule it out of order.

Mr Tony Burke (Watson, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Finance) | Hansard source

Madam Speaker, on a point of order: the issue of whether or not people are visiting workplaces was raised directly by the Prime Minister and is quite properly being addressed in this question.

Christopher Pyne (Sturt, Liberal Party, Minister for Education) | Hansard source

Madam Speaker, it was the inane remarks in the second half of the question that clearly made it into an argument. The opposition should work out how to ask a straightforward question. Therefore, I would ask you to rule it out. Maybe they could come back to it later in question time.

Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Speaker) | Hansard source

It might be helpful if the Leader of the Opposition rephrased his question.

Bill Shorten (Maribyrnong, Australian Labor Party, Leader of the Opposition) | Hansard source

Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member will resume his seat. The problem with the original question was argument contained within the question. Will the Leader of the Opposition rephrase his question to leave out that argument? Please do not restate the beginning of it; we are familiar with that. It is the end of the question that is the problem.

Bill Shorten (Maribyrnong, Australian Labor Party, Leader of the Opposition) | Hansard source

Thank you, Madam Speaker. Why won't the Prime Minister talk to Holden workers, Alcoa workers, Rio Tinto workers, Holden workers and Forge workers? Why won't he talk to them about their liberation?
