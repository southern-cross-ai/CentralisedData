House debates

Monday, 17 October 2016

Bills

Treasury Laws Amendment (Working Holiday Maker Reform) Bill 2016; Third Reading

7:25 pm

Scott Morrison (Cook, Liberal Party, Treasurer) | Hansard source

I seek leave of the House to move the third reading immediately

Leave not granted.

I move:

That so much of the standing orders be suspended as would prevent the motion for the third reading being moved without delay.

A division having been called and the bells being rung—

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for Grayndler on a point of order?

Anthony Albanese (Grayndler, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Infrastructure and Transport) | Hansard source

Mr Speaker, the Treasurer, in moving this motion, has just sought to suspend standing orders. He was not sought to invoke the contingency motions that are available to him on the Notice Paper .

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

He did not seek to suspend standing orders. He was referring to the contingency—

Anthony Albanese (Grayndler, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Infrastructure and Transport) | Hansard source

That is what he said.

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

I did not hear him say that.

Anthony Albanese (Grayndler, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Infrastructure and Transport) | Hansard source

I think you will find that he did.

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

Yes, but it is on the Notice Paper . We are proceeding. The question is that the motion moved by the Treasurer be agreed to.

7:36 pm

Scott Morrison (Cook, Liberal Party, Treasurer) | Hansard source

I move:

That this bill be now read a third time.

7:37 pm

Mr Tony Burke (Watson, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Finance) | Hansard source

I rise to speak to the motion. Those opposite are engaging in a ridiculous use of the parliament. Those opposite are shutting down a debate that can be had this week. The Senate will not sit for three weeks.

Debate interrupted.
