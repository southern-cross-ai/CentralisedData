House debates

Thursday, 7 December 2023

Statements

Personal Explanation

3:40 pm

Dan Tehan (Wannon, Liberal Party, Shadow Minister for Immigration and Citizenship) | Hansard source

Speaker, I'd like to make a personal explanation.

Milton Dick (Speaker) | Hansard source

Do you claim to have been misrepresented?

Dan Tehan (Wannon, Liberal Party, Shadow Minister for Immigration and Citizenship) | Hansard source

Most egregiously, Mr Speaker.

Milton Dick (Speaker) | Hansard source

You may proceed.

Dan Tehan (Wannon, Liberal Party, Shadow Minister for Immigration and Citizenship) | Hansard source

During question time, the Prime Minister said that I wasn't available for a legal briefing because I was at the Jericho Cup—a commemorative race meeting which honours those who have fallen in the service of duty during the First World War , including the Charge of the Light Brigade —and he said that I was playing politics.

Milton Dick (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member may resume his seat. You've corrected—

An honourable member interjecting —

Well, you've confirmed what the Prime Minister said was true, so you need to claim what the misrepresentation was.

Dan Tehan (Wannon, Liberal Party, Shadow Minister for Immigration and Citizenship) | Hansard source

He said I wasn't available to have that briefing. That briefing hadn't been offered. It was offered Sunday night—Sunday night, after the races. You—

Milton Dick (Speaker) | Hansard source

Resume your seat. The Prime Minister is seeking the call. Order!

3:41 pm

Anthony Albanese (Grayndler, Australian Labor Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

Mr Speaker, I seek leave to give a personal explanation.

Milton Dick (Speaker) | Hansard source

Do you claim to have been misrepresented?

Anthony Albanese (Grayndler, Australian Labor Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

I do, Mr Speaker. The offers were made—I go to the member for Wannon's statement—on Sunday night, Monday at 8 am, Monday at 4 pm and ever since. It is now Thursday, at 20 to four, and at no stage has the opposition taken up the opportunity of receiving access to the legal advice.
