House debates

Thursday, 1 March 2007

Nuclear Energy

Motion

9:01 am

Peter Garrett (Kingsford Smith, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Climate Change, Environment and Heritage) | Hansard source

I move:

That so much of the standing and sessional orders be suspended as would prevent the House:

(a)
noting that the Prime Minister has refused to rule out a nuclear power plant in any location in Australia;
(b)
noting the stated opposition, outside the House, of a growing number of Members to the location of a nuclear power plant in their electorates; and
(c)
providing all Members with an opportunity to come into the House and declare their opposition to the location of a nuclear power plant in their electorates.

Coalition MPs are running away from the Howard government’s plans to—

Tony Abbott (Warringah, Liberal Party, Leader of the House) | Hansard source

I move:

That the member be no longer heard.

Question put.

David Hawker (Speaker) | Hansard source

Is the motion seconded?

9:11 am

Anthony Albanese (Grayndler, Australian Labor Party, Manager of Opposition Business in the House) | Hansard source

Shine the radioactive light on them, Mr Speaker—

Tony Abbott (Warringah, Liberal Party, Leader of the House) | Hansard source

I move:

That the member be no longer heard.

Question put.

Original question put:

That the motion ( Mr Garrett’s ) be agreed to.
