House debates

Wednesday, 23 May 2012

Personal Explanations

3:23 pm

Andrew Wilkie (Denison, Independent) | Hansard source

Madam Deputy Speaker, I wish to make a personal explanation.

Ms Anna Burke (Chisholm, Deputy-Speaker) | Hansard source

Does the honourable member claim to have been misrepresented?

Andrew Wilkie (Denison, Independent) | Hansard source

Yes, Madam Deputy Speaker.

Ms Anna Burke (Chisholm, Deputy-Speaker) | Hansard source

Please proceed.

Andrew Wilkie (Denison, Independent) | Hansard source

Madam Deputy Speaker, I have been misrepresented most grievously. Yesterday in the Tasmanian House of Assembly, the Greens health spokesperson asked Premier Lara Giddings to detail how much of the $340 million I secured for the Royal Hobart Hospital would be deducted from Tasmania's future GST allocation. The Premier is reported as saying $88 million will be clawed back each year for three years, which would amount to a total of $264 million. This figure is a fantasy, apparently concocted by the Tasmanian Labor government to shift blame to me for the dreadful state of the Tasmanian budget, a fantasy perpetuated by the Greens who do not want the hospital to be rebuilt and are prepared to play political games with public health policy.

Ms Anna Burke (Chisholm, Deputy-Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for Denison must go to where he has been misrepresented.

Andrew Wilkie (Denison, Independent) | Hansard source

Madam Deputy Speaker, the fact is that the Commonwealth Grants Commission , not the Premier of Tasmania , is the authority on this matter and in its report this year the commission identified a GST clawback of $59 million. Future figures are unknown although the federal Treasury advises most of the $340 million will be new money.

Ms Anna Burke (Chisholm, Deputy-Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for Denison is now debating the issue.

Andrew Wilkie (Denison, Independent) | Hansard source

Thank you, Madam Deputy Speaker.
