Statements by Members

Australian National Flag Day

1:46 pm

Julie Owens (Parramatta, Australian Labor Party) | Hansard source

It was a pleasure on Monday last week to celebrate Australian National Flag Day with a number of my local schools. Parramatta High School has hosted the National Flag Day ceremony for surrounding schools since 2001. The day commemorates that day back on 3 September 1901 when the Australian flag design was first displayed following a competition that attracted some 33,000 entries. Given that I was commemorating the day with school students, I thought it was worth mentioning that of the five original prizewinners in that flag competition in 1901 two were teenagers—a schoolboy, Ivor Evans , and an apprentice optician, Leslie Hawkins .

Last Monday the flag was raised by three very able flag bearers—James Feng, Anthony Liu and Mohammed Arif, who did a fine job. Also in attendance were Ali Al Zaidi, Meghna Alapatt and John Pinar Akin from Parramatta High School ; Dwayne Medoca from Parramatta Public School; Alfred Aye and Ebru Sazak from Parramatta West Public School; Abbinesh Laguthaas and Ramya Sutheskumar from Westmead Public School ; and Jack Morgan and Teannie Makdesi from Hilltop Road Public School. As in every year, they were joined by representatives from Legacy and the Parramatta RSL sub-branch. It was a great day once again and I thank them all for helping to celebrate National Flag Day.
