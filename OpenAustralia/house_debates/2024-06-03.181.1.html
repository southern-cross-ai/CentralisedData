Statements by Members

Werriwa electorate: Liverpool Catholic Club

4:16 pm

Anne Stanley (Werriwa, Australian Labor Party) | Hansard source

It was a great pleasure to be present at the annual Liverpool Catholic Club mass and luncheon. Now in its 53rd year, the date was an appropriate celebration of the club, its members and the support it provides to our community. The club supports local schools, 12 intraclubs and provides grants to groups like the Autism Advisory and Support Service, the Ingham institute and Youth off the Streets. These and many others are provided with much-needed money. In fact, the club makes more that $2 million a year available to groups like the Liverpool Women's Health Centre, Middleton Grange Rural Fire Brigade, junior cricket clubs, and scholarships for local schools.

The day also recognises HSC high achievers, dux of schools, trade and arts achievements. The support provided assists with tuition fees and with buying materials to make the transition to tertiary education easier for students. The club honoured Phil Coleman's retirement from the board after 42 years. Mr Coleman has actively supported our community all of that time, including seven years as president. Father Paul Monkerud, Episcopal Vicar for Western region also celebrated a milestone birthday. I recognise all the club does to support our community. It's a wonderful treasure for all of our volunteers and the support they provide is fantastic.
