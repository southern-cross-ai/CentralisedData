House debates

Questions without Notice

Pauline Hanson's One Nation

2:22 pm

Chris Bowen (McMahon, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Treasurer) | Hansard source

My question is to the Prime Minister. Will the government reveal the details of its secret deal with One Nation before the 28 July by-elections or after them?

2:23 pm

Malcolm Turnbull (Wentworth, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

Talking about personal discussions and conversations, I noted in the shadow minister for small business's interview with Alan Jones today, he was slipping and sliding, failing to defend his leader's captain's call—

Ms Rowland interjecting —

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for Greenway is warned.

Malcolm Turnbull (Wentworth, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

And he said:

Just on your point, Alan, while I'm not going to go into personal conversations that the leadership group or Bill has with us or I have with him …

Why not? But he says he always takes a very consultative approach. There'll be nothing more consultative, I would say, than the event in Devonport on 4 July, when the Leader of the Opposition is having a business luncheon and is going to reveal Labor's plan for Braddon. It's advertised nicely. There's the Leader of the Opposition, and there are two young men in hard hats and high-vis. I'm just wondering who they work for. Do you think they might be working for Penguin Composites, so popular—

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

The Prime Minister will resume his seat. The Manager of Opposition Business on a point of order.

Mr Tony Burke (Watson, Australian Labor Party, Manager of Opposition Business (House)) | Hansard source

Direct relevance. It's a really—if yesterday was a long bow, this one's extraordinary.
