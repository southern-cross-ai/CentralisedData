House debates

Wednesday, 20 June 2007

Questions without Notice

Liberal Party

2:25 pm

Bob McMullan (Fraser, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Federal/State Relations) | Hansard source

My question is addressed to the Prime Minister. I refer to the joint operational arrangements for the BCA - ACCI campaign and specifically to the fact that the campaign director is chief Liberal strategist Mark Textor , the operations director is former senior Liberal Mr Tony Barry and the campaign committee includes former Liberal advisers Peter Anderson and Brett Hogan. I also refer to the fact that Crosby Textor, run by former Liberal Party director Lynton Crosby , is conducting the campaign. What guarantees can the Prime Minister provide that these Liberal Party operatives have not and will not have access to taxpayer funded opinion poll research?

John Howard (Bennelong, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

The government will not be funding any business campaigns any more than we will be funding any union campaigns.

Brendan O'Connor (Gorton, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Parliamentary Secretary for Industrial Relations) | Hansard source

Mr Brendan O’Connor interjecting

David Hawker (Speaker) | Hansard source

Order! The member for Gorton is warned!

John Howard (Bennelong, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

The member allows me to make this comparison: the big difference with the links between the Australian Labor Party and the union movement is that the union movement run the Australian Labor Party. They have delegates at major party conferences. The campaign director for the union movement’s campaign—

Wayne Swan (Lilley, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Treasurer) | Hansard source

I rise on a point of order, which goes to relevance.

John Howard (Bennelong, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

Hello, we don’t like this, do we!

Wayne Swan (Lilley, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Treasurer) | Hansard source

The question was about taxpayer funded research.

David Hawker (Speaker) | Hansard source

I remind the member for Lilley that the member for Fraser asked a fairly long question and the Prime Minister is responding to that question.

John Howard (Bennelong, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

It is well known that members of the trade union movement dominate ALP conferences. Members of the Business Council are not delegates to Liberal Party conferences. The campaign director for the trade union movement against Work Choices is none other than the Labor Party candidate for what is the seat of Shortland in the upcoming federal election. Any attempt to draw some kind of link between the Liberal Party and this campaign is absolute nonsense.

David Hawker (Speaker) | Hansard source

Has the Prime Minister completed his answer?

John Howard (Bennelong, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

Yes, I have.

Stephen Smith (Perth, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Education and Training) | Hansard source

I rise on a point of order. All he had to do was tell us when he handed over the research.

David Hawker (Speaker) | Hansard source

That is not a point of order.
