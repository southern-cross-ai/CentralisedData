House debates

Tuesday, 24 February 2015

Questions without Notice

Australian Human Rights Commission

2:11 pm

Bill Shorten (Maribyrnong, Australian Labor Party, Leader of the Opposition) | Hansard source

My question is to the Prime Minister. Was the Prime Minister's office aware that the Attorney-General was asking the President of the Australian Human Rights Commission to resign and offering her another job in order to persuade her to resign?

Tony Abbott (Warringah, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

I do not presume to be across what may or may not have been said in evidence in Senate estimates today.

Ms Claydon interjecting —

Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for Newcastle is not in her seat and will desist!

Tony Abbott (Warringah, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

I do not claim to be across what may or may not have been canvassed between the President of the Human Rights Commission and the Attorney or indeed any other member of this government. All I know is that this government has lost confidence in the President of the Human Rights Commission. I believe the Australian people have lost confidence in the President of the Human Rights Commission, because any person who seriously recommends that a convicted murderer should be paid $300,000 in compensation for being in immigration detention has simply—

Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Speaker) | Hansard source

The Prime Minister will resume his seat.

Mr Tony Burke (Watson, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Finance) | Hansard source

Madam Speaker, I—

Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Speaker) | Hansard source

I call the honourable member for Mitchell.
