Wednesday, 4 May 2016

Statements by Members

Taxation

1:59 pm

Kevin Hogan (Page, National Party) | Hansard source

Multinationals need to pay their fair share of tax. The new diverted profit tax announced last night will make sure large multinationals are paying the tax they should on profits made in Australia. I have always believed, as does my community, that, if you make a profit in Australia, that profit should be taxed in Australia. The people in my community of Page work hard and pay their taxes; so should large multinationals. This new diverted profits tax will result in many billions of dollars to the Australian community. I am proud that this government will close the loopholes that allow large corporations to exploit the differences between Australia's and other countries' tax rates. It will start on 1 July this year. We will provide the ATO with greater powers to deal with these multinationals. They will be penalised at a 40 per cent tax rate.
