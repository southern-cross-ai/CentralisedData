House debates

Monday, 22 September 2014

Questions without Notice

Indigenous Affairs

3:06 pm

Bill Shorten (Maribyrnong, Australian Labor Party, Leader of the Opposition) | Hansard source

My question is to the Prime Minister. This month I visited the Bubup Wilam Early Learning Centre in Thomastown. It is a centre that provides vital early education help and family support services for vulnerable Indigenous children and families. This centre is now one of 38 centres across Australia facing closure because of the Prime Minister's cuts to Indigenous services. Why should the children who go there, and their parents, pay for the Prime Minister's broken promises.

Tony Abbott (Warringah, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

I fear that members opposite are in fact misleading the House in this area. I really do.

Opposition Members:

Opposition members interjecting —

Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Speaker) | Hansard source

There will be silence on my left.

Mr Shorten interjecting —

The Leader of the Opposition will desist.

Tony Abbott (Warringah, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

My recollection of this is that the former government did make capital funding available for Indigenous childcare centres.

Ms Kate Ellis interjecting —

Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for Adelaide is warned.

Tony Abbott (Warringah, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

It made no recurrent funding available and the capital funding came out, as I understand it, on 1 July this year. So members opposite, when they were in government, made capital funding available. The capital funding that they made available ceased, as I understand it, on 1 July and they never made recurrent funding available because, quite properly, they thought recurrent funding for preschools should be provided by state and territory governments. I know they have got—

Mr Shorten interjecting —

Mr Hockey interjecting —

Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Speaker) | Hansard source

The Leader of the Opposition and the Treasurer will desist.

Tony Abbott (Warringah, Liberal Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

Members opposite cannot help themselves. They think that more money is the answer to every single problem. The reality is that if there have been any cuts in this particular area, they are cuts put in place by the former government.
