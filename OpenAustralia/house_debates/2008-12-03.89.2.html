House debates

Wednesday, 3 December 2008

Personal Explanations

3:44 pm

Christopher Pyne (Sturt, Liberal Party, Shadow Minister for Education, Apprenticeships and Training) | Hansard source

Mr Speaker, I wish to make a personal explanation.

Harry Jenkins (Speaker) | Hansard source

Does the honourable member claim to have been misrepresented?

Christopher Pyne (Sturt, Liberal Party, Shadow Minister for Education, Apprenticeships and Training) | Hansard source

Most grievously.

Harry Jenkins (Speaker) | Hansard source

Please proceed.

Christopher Pyne (Sturt, Liberal Party, Shadow Minister for Education, Apprenticeships and Training) | Hansard source

In question time, the Minister for Education asserted that the opposition had no support for its position on the Schools Assistance Bill. It is quite the opposite, and I seek leave to table a small selection of the emails and letters that I have had in the last couple of hours from school principals all across Australia, supporting the stance of the opposition—from schools like St Michael ’s Grammar School , the Free Reformed School Association, Fitzroy Community School—

Harry Jenkins (Speaker) | Hansard source

Order! The member for Sturt knows he cannot debate the issue.

Christopher Pyne (Sturt, Liberal Party, Shadow Minister for Education, Apprenticeships and Training) | Hansard source

Melbourne Grammar School , the Victorian Parents Council, the Castlemaine Steiner School. Do I need to go on, Mr Speaker? Have I made my point?

Harry Jenkins (Speaker) | Hansard source

No. The member has sought leave to table documents. Is leave granted? Leave is not granted. The member for Sturt will resume his seat.
