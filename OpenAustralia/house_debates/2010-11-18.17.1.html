House debates

Thursday, 18 November 2010

Same-Sex Marriage

Debate resumed from 15 November, on motion by Mr Bandt :

That this House:

(1)
notes that there is:
(a)
a growing list of countries that allow same-sex couples to marry including the Netherlands, Belgium, Norway, Spain, Canada and South Africa; and
(b)
widespread support for equal marriage in the Australian community; and
(2)
calls on all parliamentarians to gauge their constituents’ views on the issue of marriage equality.

upon which Mr Stephen Jones moved by way of amendment:

That all words after ‘That’ be omitted with a view to substituting the following words: ‘this House calls on all parliamentarians, consistent with their duties as representatives, to gauge their constituents’ views on ways to achieve equal treatment for same sex couples including marriage.’

Question put:

That the words proposed to be omitted ( Mr Stephen Jones’s amendment) stand part of the question.

Question put:

That the words proposed to be inserted be so inserted.

Question put:

That the motion ( Mr Bandt’s ), as amended, be agreed to.
