House debates

Wednesday, 4 May 2016

Personal Explanations

9:01 am

Jenny Macklin (Jagajaga, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Families and Payments) | Hansard source

Mr Speaker, I wish to make a personal explanation.

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

Does the member for Jagajaga claim to have been misrepresented?

Jenny Macklin (Jagajaga, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Families and Payments) | Hansard source

I do, Mr Speaker.

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for Jagajaga may proceed.

Jenny Macklin (Jagajaga, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Families and Payments) | Hansard source

I claim to have been misrepresented in the parliament yesterday by the Prime Minister, the Deputy Prime Minister, the Minister for Social Services , the Minister for Industry, Innovation and Science, the member for Ryan and the member for Solomon, who all wrongly accused me of using the term 'rounding error'. I make it very clear to the House that this is completely false. I never said any such thing. They all made it up and they should all come in here and apologise to the House for misleading this parliament.

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for Jagajaga will resume her seat. She has made her personal explanation.

9:02 am

Chris Bowen (McMahon, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Treasurer) | Hansard source

Mr Speaker, I wish to make a personal explanation.

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

Does the member for McMahon claim to have been misrepresented?

Chris Bowen (McMahon, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Treasurer) | Hansard source

I certainly do, Mr Speaker.

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

The member for McMahon may proceed.

Chris Bowen (McMahon, Australian Labor Party, Shadow Treasurer) | Hansard source

Yesterday the following government members claimed that I used the term 'rounding error': the Treasurer, the member for Mitchell and Senator Scott Ryan. That is a complete fabrication. I said nothing even vaguely like that. Those members should withdraw.

Tony Smith (Speaker) | Hansard source

I thank the member for McMahon.
