House debates

Wednesday, 28 May 2008

Questions without Notice

Fuel Prices

2:39 pm

Brendan Nelson (Bradfield, Liberal Party, Leader of the Opposition) | Hansard source

My question is to the Prime Minister. Why won’t the Prime Minister guarantee that Australians won’t pay a cent more on petrol under Fuelwatch?

Kevin Rudd (Griffith, Australian Labor Party, Prime Minister) | Hansard source

The former Liberal Prime Minister of Australia stood here with the support of all those opposite and not only said that working families had never been better off but went one better and said irresponsibly that interest rates would be kept at record lows. If I know one thing about the Australian people it is that they are sick and tired of politicians making irresponsible promises. We have introduced a competition policy measure. We have said that it is not a silver bullet but we think that it is the right way ahead. At least we have a policy. Those opposite, as of today, have four different policies. It would be useful if the opposition would provide clarity on that.
