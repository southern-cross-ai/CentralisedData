Report summarising the petitions and Ministerial responses being presented.

The committee met in private session in the 47th Parliament on 8 February 2023, 15 February 2023 and 8 March 2023.

1. The committee resolved to present the following 18 p etitions in accordance with standing order 207:

From 407 petitioners—requesting that Trove is kept as a free and open service to all Australians (EN4755)

From 5 petitioners—requesting the temporary postponement of implementation of revised pet import regulations (EN4756)

From 12 petitioners—requesting the electrification of Australia's freight lines (EN4759)

From 489 petitioners—requesting that Trove be fully funded (EN4761)

From 14 petitioners—requesting military support for Ukraine (EN4763)

From 17 petitioners—requesting the appointment of independent board members to Screen Australia (EN4764)

From 3 petitioners—requesting removal of requirements on student and work visas (EN4765)

From 39 petitioners—requesting to change the national anthem to We Are Australian (EN4766)

From 36 petitioners—requesting parliamentary debate prior to the indigenous voice-to-parliament referendum (EN4769)

From 8 petitioners—requesting that people are allowed to donate money to the Commonwealth of Australia (EN4772)

From 26 petitioners—requesting a change of Australia Day to the last Friday of January (EN4773)

From 69 petitioners—requesting a ban on companies who have convictions for illegal research (EN4775)

From 83 petitioners—requesting a freeze on land and businesses being bought by foreign entities (EN4776)

From 68 petitioners—requesting a ban on the sale of energy drinks to children (EN4778)

From 182 petitioners—requesting a change to the 491 visa (EN4780)

From 47 petitioners—requesting cancellation of the cashless debit card (EN4783)

From 5506 petitioners—requesting permanent residency for those who came by boat 2012-2014 (EN4784)

From 2897 petitioners—requesting continuing funding for "sealing the hills" project (EN4785)
