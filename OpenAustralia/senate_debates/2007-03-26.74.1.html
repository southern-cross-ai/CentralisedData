Senate debates

Monday, 26 March 2007

World Day for Water

3:41 pm

Rachel Siewert (Australian Greens) | Hansard source

I move:

That the Senate:
(a)
notes:
(i)
that Thursday, 22 March 2007, has been designated World Day for Water 2007, and that this year’s theme is ‘Coping with Water Scarcity ’,
(ii)
that the South Australia n Government has indicated that it will cut water flows to nine key lakes, wetlands and lagoons if its water allocation falls below 50 per cent or weir levels cannot be sustained, and
(iii)
the comments by Murray-Darling Basin Commission Chief Executive Wendy Craik, that climate change will have significant long-term impacts on inflows into the Murray-Darling river system; and
(b)
calls on the Government to ensure that:
(i)
water allocations are acquired such that supplies to wetlands in South Australia, including Lake Bonney , Gurra Gurra Lakes, Horseshoe Lagoon, Ross Lagoon and Murbko South Wetland are maintained, and
(ii)
water management plans in the Murray-Darling Basin are consistent with sustainable extraction levels and can take into account projections of reduced inflows into the basin due to climate change.

Question put.
