Questions without Notice

Taxation

2:00 pm

Doug Cameron (Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Human Services) | Hansard source

My question is to the Minister representing the Prime Minister, Senator Brandis. Last year, the minister advised the Senate:

There will be no change to the 50 per cent CGT discount for individuals.

Does this remain the Turnbull government's position?

George Brandis (Liberal Party, Attorney-General) | Hansard source

The government has no intention of supporting Labor's policies on negative gearing or capital gains tax. The government is focused on returning the budget to balance and addressing the cost pressures on households and business, in particular, on energy and child care.

Stephen Parry (President) | Hansard source

Senator Cameron, a supplementary question.

Doug Cameron (Australian Labor Party, Shadow Minister for Human Services) | Hansard source

You say you have no intention, but does that include no intention in the May budget? Will there be any changes to the capital gains tax in the May budget?
