Senate debates

Tuesday, 28 November 2017

Questions without Notice

Broadband

2:07 pm

Deborah O'Neill (Australian Labor Party) | Hansard source

My question is to the Minister for Communications, Senator Fifield. Yesterday, NBN Co announced an immediate halt in the rollout of its second-rate HFC cable because it's unable to deliver a reliable, quality service. When did the minister first become aware that NBN was preparing to halt the HFC rollout? When did the minister inform Mr Turnbull of the delays? And why was this kept secret until yesterday?

Mitch Fifield (Liberal Party, Manager of Government Business in the Senate) | Hansard source

I thank the senator for her question. NBN advised me on Wednesday the 22nd that they were looking to have a pause. As you'd be aware, Senator, section 91 of the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act states that the directors of a wholly-owned Commonwealth company must give the responsible minister reasonable notice if the directors become aware of any significant issue that may affect the company or any of its subsidiaries. Such requirements were put in place to ensure that shareholder ministers and their departments have adequate notice to assess the implications of issues raised by a government business enterprise. As colleagues are aware, NBN made an announcement yesterday.

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

Senator O'Neill , a supplementary question?

Deborah O'Neill (Australian Labor Party) | Hansard source

No; a point of order.

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

Has the minister concluded his answer?

Deborah O'Neill (Australian Labor Party) | Hansard source

Minister, you didn't answer the question with regard to when Prime Minister Turnbull was made aware of the delays.

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

Sorry, Senator O'Neill . I wasn't sure if Senator Fifield sat in conclusion or if he sat when you stood. He sat in conclusion. Senator O'Neill, you have a supplementary question.

2:08 pm

Deborah O'Neill (Australian Labor Party) | Hansard source

I note that the minister didn't answer the question relating to when he advised Mr Turnbull of the delays. In May, the NBN Co confirmed that a seven-month delay to the activation of three million HFC premises would add $1 billion to NBN funding requirements. What impact will these most recent delays have on the NBN funding requirements?

2:09 pm

Mitch Fifield (Liberal Party, Manager of Government Business in the Senate) | Hansard source

NBN is still assessing these matters and is still to provide advice to government. As I mentioned, the section 91 notice is intended to give the opportunity to shareholder ministers to ask questions. We took the decision with NBN Co that it was appropriate for the public to be advised sooner, and that is what happened. We are still awaiting further advice from NBN Co.

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

A final supplementary question, Senator O'Neill .

2:10 pm

Deborah O'Neill (Australian Labor Party) | Hansard source

Given it was Mr Turnbull who spearheaded the shift to his so-called superior technology of HFC before the last election, doesn't the responsibility of this mess lie solely in the hands of the current Prime Minister?

Opposition Senators:

Opposition senators interjecting —

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

Order! We can wait until there's some quiet before I call Senator Fifield to answer. Senator Fifield.

Mitch Fifield (Liberal Party, Manager of Government Business in the Senate) | Hansard source

The multitechnology mix is one of the reasons the NBN will be completed by 2020—six to eight years sooner than would have been the case under those opposite. HFC is and will remain an important technology for the NBN. As the CEO of NBN Co has identified, there are some issues that are being addressed, which are readily fixable, to do with interference in terms of spectrum and also to do with some of the joints between the cable in the street and the cable that goes to the premises. These are all very fixable. HFC is a good product—

Senator Wong interjecting —

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

Order! Senator Fifield, Senator Macdonald is on his feet on a point of order.

Ian Macdonald (Liberal Party) | Hansard source

The Leader of the Opposition in the Senate seems to think that she is able to interject consistently and continually, drowning out the minister so that I can't hear. Can you please tell her that she is a senator and is obliged to follow the same rules as every other senator.

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

I will ask all senators to respect their colleagues and restrain themselves from interjections. Senator Fifield.

Mitch Fifield (Liberal Party, Manager of Government Business in the Senate) | Hansard source

As I was about to say, the majority of people in the United States who have broadband receive it over the HFC pay TV cable.

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

Have you concluded, Senator Fifield? On a point of order, Senator O'Neill .

Deborah O'Neill (Australian Labor Party) | Hansard source

There are only eight seconds remaining, and the minister still has not addressed the question I asked him: does the responsibility for the mess of the HFC rollout rest solely in the hands of the Prime Minister? We haven't gone anywhere near that.

A government senator interjecting —

Scott Ryan (President, Special Minister of State) | Hansard source

Order—on my right, on this occasion.

An opposition senator interjecting —

Now on my left—order! Senator O'Neil, the minister was being directly relevant to the question. I would ask senators, when they raise a point of order on direct relevance, to find more creative ways to bring attention to it rather than simply restating the question, which is not in order. Senator Fifield, in conclusion.

Mitch Fifield (Liberal Party, Manager of Government Business in the Senate) | Hansard source

I should indicate that a large number of people in HFC areas on the pre- NBN network can access broadband over the HFC.
